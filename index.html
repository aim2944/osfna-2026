Our Story

30 Years of Oromo Heritage

In the early 1990s, visionary members of the Oromo diaspora conceived an ambitious idea — the creation of an Oromo Sports Federation in North America. That vision took tangible form in 1996 when the inaugural OSFNA soccer tournament launched with six founding clubs. Today, after three decades of growth, OSFNA is the premier gathering of the Oromo diaspora across the continent — a week-long celebration of sport, culture, and community unity recognized by both the Minneapolis and Saint Paul mayors' offices as Oromo Week.

"Both the Minneapolis and Saint Paul mayors' offices have recognized OSFNA Sports and Oromo Cultural Festival Week as Oromo Week."

Oromo Week 2026

Beyond the pitch, Oromo Week is a full week of celebration — nightlife, daytime activities, cultural showcases, and community gatherings. This is where the Oromo diaspora comes home.

👑
Miss Oromo North America
Running since 2003, this beloved annual program celebrates Oromo women and culture. MONA 2025 crowned Ofifan Dabala.
🎵
Nightly Concerts
Star-studded concerts featuring renowned Oromo artists every night. Past performers include Gutu Abera, Kemer Yusuf, and Wako Getacho.
🎭
Cultural Performances
Traditional Oromo dance, music, and cultural exhibitions performed before matches and during halftime.
☀️
Daytime Activities
Community forums, youth programming, exhibitions, and family-friendly events for all ages.
🌐

Frequently Asked Questions

When does registration open?

June 1, 2026. Early registration (before May 31) receives Late registration July 11–20.

Where is OSFNA 2026 being held?

OSFNA 2026 takes place in Minneapolis, Minnesota. Final venue-specific addresses will be posted on the schedule and passport pages as they are finalized.

How many players can be on a roster?

Soccer: up to 22 players (min 12). Basketball: up to 12 (min 8). Golf: 4-person teams.

Can I vendor or sponsor OSFNA 2026?

Yes! Vendor applications open April 2026. Email myosfna@gmail.com to discuss sponsorship.

Is OSFNA open to non-Oromo players?

All athletes are welcome. Each team must have at least 50% Oromo players per OSFNA bylaws.
