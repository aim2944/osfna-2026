Our Story

Rooted in
Oromo Heritage

In the early 1990s, a group of visionary members of the Oromo diaspora residing in Toronto, Canada, conceived an ambitious idea: the creation of an Oromo Sports Federation in North America. This concept, born out of a desire to foster community cohesion and celebrate Oromo heritage, took tangible form in 1996 when the inaugural OSFNA soccer tournament was launched.

Six clubs eagerly participated in that landmark event — Rissa (Minnesota), Madaa Walabuu (Seattle), Oromia (Toronto), Odaa (Canada), Barisaa (Atlanta), and Bakalcha (Ottawa). The tournament showcased talent and passion for soccer while serving as a rallying point for cultural exchange and unity.

The enthusiastic response from clubs and fans alike demonstrated growing momentum. In 1997, the second tournament heralded a promising future — necessitating the formal establishment of the OSFNA Board through democratic elections, marking a significant milestone in the organization's journey.

"Both the Minneapolis and Saint Paul mayors' offices have recognized OSFNA Sports and Oromo Cultural Festival Week as Oromo Week."

OSFNA is a nonprofit organization registered in the State of Minnesota, USA. Since 1996, it has consistently captivated the Oromo diaspora — offering a rich tapestry of cultural and sporting events that help Oromos reconnect with their roots, celebrate their identity, and forge lasting bonds across borders.

Culture & Community

Oromo Week

Beyond the pitch, Oromo Week is a full week of celebration — nightlife, daytime activities, cultural showcases, and community gatherings organized by OSFNA, public entities, and private enterprises. This is where the Oromo diaspora comes home.

👑
Miss Oromo North America
Running since 2003, this beloved annual program celebrates Oromo women and culture. MONA 2025 crowned Ofifan Dabala, with special recognition to Mergitu Argo for 23 years of organizing the program.
🎵
Nightly Concerts
Star-studded concerts featuring renowned Oromo artists every night of Oromo Week. Past performers include Gutu Abera, Kemer Yusuf, and Wako Getacho.
🎭
Cultural Performances
Traditional Oromo dance, music, and cultural exhibitions performed before matches and during halftime — a living showcase of Oromo identity and heritage.
☀️
Daytime Activities
Oromo Week fills every hour — community forums, youth programming, exhibitions, and family-friendly daytime events for all ages.
🌐
Community Forums
Panel discussions, heritage exhibitions, and community organizing sessions that connect Oromos across North America around shared goals and culture.
🛍️
Vendors & Marketplace
OSFNA collaborates with local businesses to offer merchandise, food, and services. Oromo entrepreneurs and local vendors unite during the festival.
