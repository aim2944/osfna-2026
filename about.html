Our Story

30 Years of Oromo Heritage, Sport & Community

The OSFNA Story

In the early 1990s, a group of visionary members of the Oromo diaspora residing in Toronto, Canada, conceived an ambitious idea: the creation of an Oromo Sports Federation in North America. This concept, born out of a desire to foster community cohesion and celebrate Oromo heritage, took tangible form in 1996 when the inaugural OSFNA soccer tournament was launched with just six founding clubs.

Six clubs eagerly participated in that landmark event — Rissa (Minnesota), Madaa Walabuu (Seattle), Oromia (Toronto), Odaa (Canada), Barisaa (Atlanta), and Bakalcha (Ottawa). The tournament showcased talent and passion for soccer while serving as a rallying point for cultural exchange, community building, and unity across borders.

The enthusiastic response from clubs and fans alike demonstrated growing momentum. In 1997, the second tournament heralded a promising future — necessitating the formal establishment of the OSFNA Board through democratic elections. This marked a significant milestone, transforming OSFNA from a single event into a sustainable institution.

"Both the Minneapolis and Saint Paul mayors' offices have recognized OSFNA Sports and Oromo Cultural Festival Week as Oromo Week."

Over three decades, OSFNA has evolved into the premier Oromo cultural and athletic gathering in North America. What began as a single soccer tournament now encompasses multiple sports, cultural performances, community forums, and celebrations that attract thousands of Oromos from across the continent. Today, OSFNA is a nonprofit organization registered in Minnesota, USA.

OSFNA's mission remains constant: to celebrate Oromo heritage through sport and culture, strengthen diaspora connections, foster youth development, and create lasting bonds across North America. Every year, families, athletes, and community leaders gather for Oromo Week — a vibrant testament to the resilience, unity, and pride of the Oromo people.
